# Rocket Launch Emissions vs. Reentry Emissions  
*(English Version — Public-Friendly Edition)*

---

## 1. What This Topic Is About
This document explains the difference between **rocket launch pollution** and **satellite reentry pollution**, and why both matter for the atmosphere.

Many people know rockets pollute.  
Fewer people know **reentering satellites also pollute**, and in a very different (and sometimes more dangerous) way.

This section gives a simple, public-friendly explanation.

---

## 2. Rocket Launch Emissions (Simple Explanation)

### Rockets release pollution during ascent:
Depending on fuel type, they emit:

- **Black carbon (soot)**  
  → absorbs sunlight, warms the atmosphere  
- **NOx (nitrogen oxides)**  
- **Aluminum particles** (for solid boosters)  
- **Chlorine compounds** (older fuels)  
- **Water vapor at high altitude**  
- **CO₂** from hydrocarbon fuels  

### Where does rocket pollution go?
- Mostly into the **stratosphere and mesosphere**
- Most particles settle within **months**
- Black carbon stays **1–3 years**
- But launches are still relatively few in number  

### Main concerns:
- Soot heats the upper atmosphere  
- Harmful chemicals damage the ozone layer  
- Water vapor influences cloud formation  

Rocket pollution is short-term but **intense at the moment of launch**.

---

## 3. Reentry Emissions (Simple Explanation)

When satellites fall back to Earth:

- They burn at ~7,000°C  
- Aluminum turns into **Al₂O₃ nanoparticles**  
- These tiny particles accumulate in the stratosphere

### Why is reentry more dangerous long-term?
- These particles **stay 5–10 years**  
- They do not break down  
- They trigger **ozone-destroying reactions**  
- More satellites = more pollution every year  
- Mega-constellations multiply the problem  

**Reentry pollution is long-lasting and increasing rapidly**, unlike rocket launches.

---

## 4. Launch vs. Reentry — Simple Comparison Table

| Category | Rocket Launch Emissions | Satellite Reentry Emissions |
|---------|--------------------------|------------------------------|
| Main Pollutants | Soot, NOx, chlorine, alumina | Al₂O₃ nanoparticles |
| Duration | Months to a few years | 5–10 years |
| Trend | Slowly increasing | Growing very fast |
| Impact on Ozone | Medium–High | Medium–High |
| Climate Impact | Regional heating | Radiative forcing changes |
| Control Options | Better fuels, cleaner engines | Better satellite materials, controlled disposal |

---

## 5. What Scientists Are Worried About

### Rocket launches  
If launch rates grow 10×, soot could meaningfully change upper-atmosphere heating patterns.

### Reentry  
If thousands of satellites burn up yearly, Al₂O₃ accumulation may become comparable to volcanic aerosol levels in parts of the atmosphere.

Both together create an **atmospheric load Earth has never experienced**.

# 火箭排放 vs. 再入排放  
*(中文版本 — 大眾閱讀版)*

---

## 1. 這個主題在討論什麼  
本文件解釋「火箭發射的污染」與「衞星再入的污染」兩者的差異，以及為何兩者都對大氣層造成壓力。

大多數人知道火箭會污染。  
但 **衞星再入造成的污染其實更嚴重，而且更持久**。

這裡用最簡單的方式解釋。

---

## 2. 火箭發射污染（簡化說明）

### 火箭在上升時會排放：

- **黑碳（soot）**  
  → 強吸熱，提高大氣溫度  
- **NOx 氮氧化物**  
- **鋁顆粒**（固態火箭）  
- **氯化物**（舊式燃料）  
- **高空水氣**  
- **CO₂**（碳氫燃料）  

### 火箭污染去哪裡？
- 主要進入 **平流層與中層大氣**  
- 大部分數月內沉降  
- 黑碳可停留 **1–3 年**  
- 發射量仍在可控範圍  

### 主要問題：
- 高空升溫  
- 臭氧層化學破壞  
- 影響雲層形成  

火箭污染屬於 **短期但瞬間強烈** 的影響。

---

## 3. 再入污染（簡化說明）

當衞星再入：

- 高達 7,000°C  
- 鋁燃燒產生 **Al₂O₃ 微粒**  
- 微粒上升停留在平流層多年  

### 為什麼再入更危險？
- 微粒可停留 **5–10 年**  
- 不會分解  
- 會催化臭氧破壞  
- 衞星愈多，污染愈大  
- 星鏈化讓污染成長速度倍增  

**再入污染是長期、累積、且不斷變嚴重。**

---

## 4. 火箭 vs. 再入 — 簡化比較表

| 項目 | 火箭發射排放 | 衞星再入排放 |
|------|----------------|----------------|
| 主要污染 | 黑碳、NOx、氯、鋁 | 氧化鋁微粒 |
| 停留時間 | 數月～數年 | 5～10 年 |
| 成長趨勢 | 緩慢成長 | 急速上升 |
| 臭氧破壞 | 中～高 | 中～高 |
| 氣候影響 | 高空升溫 | 輻射平衡改變 |
| 可控方式 | 乾淨燃料、改善引擎 | 改良衛星材料、改用可控處理方式 |

---

## 5. 科學家擔心什麼？

### 火箭  
若發射率提升 10 倍，高空升溫與臭氧層破壞可能明顯增加。

### 再入  
若每年數千衞星燒蝕，大氣層中 Al₂O₃ 濃度將達前所未見的規模。

兩者一起造成的負荷，是地球過去數十萬年從未承受過的。
