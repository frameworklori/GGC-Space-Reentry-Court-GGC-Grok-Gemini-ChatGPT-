# Atmospheric Monitoring Responsibility Model  
*(English Version — Public-Friendly Edition)*

---

## 1. What This Topic Is About
This document explains **who should be responsible** for monitoring pollution caused by satellite reentry.

Currently:
- No country is legally required to monitor Al₂O₃ in the stratosphere.
- No global agency tracks reentry emissions.
- Satellite companies are not obligated to provide data.

This is a **global blind spot**.

---

## 2. Why Monitoring Is Important
You cannot regulate what you cannot measure.

Without monitoring:
- We don’t know how much Al₂O₃ is in the atmosphere.
- We don’t know how fast pollution is increasing.
- We cannot estimate ozone loss accurately.
- We cannot assign responsibility to operators.

A monitoring system is essential before any global rules or limits can exist.

---

## 3. What Should Be Monitored
A complete system needs to track:

### ● 1. Amount of Al₂O₃ emitted  
Each reentry should be estimated based on mass and materials.

### ● 2. Particle concentration in the stratosphere  
Measured using ground LIDAR, balloons, or satellite instruments.

### ● 3. Ozone thickness  
To understand chemical impacts.

### ● 4. Radiative forcing effects  
How particles change heat absorption and reflectivity.

### ● 5. Reentry location and frequency  
To identify “pollution hotspots.”

---

## 4. Who Should Be Responsible? (Simple Framework)

| Sector | Responsibility |
|--------|----------------|
| **Satellite Operators** | Provide satellite material data, end-of-life logs, reentry timing |
| **National Governments** | Monitor their airspace, enforce emission rules |
| **Space Agencies (NASA/ESA/JAXA)** | Build scientific instruments and models |
| **International Bodies (UN COPUOS)** | Set global standards and reporting rules |
| **Independent Scientists** | Validate transparency and accuracy |

This model ensures accountability at every level.

---

## 5. Tools for Monitoring
A realistic monitoring system could use:

### ✔ Ground-based LIDAR  
Measures particle density at high altitude.

### ✔ High-altitude balloons  
Direct sampling of aerosols.

### ✔ Satellite-based sensors  
Global coverage for ozone and radiation balance.

### ✔ Mandatory operator reporting  
A simple rule:  
**If you put a satellite in space, you must report what it releases when it returns.**

# 大氣監測責任模型  
*(中文版本 — 大眾閱讀版)*

---

## 1. 這個主題在討論什麼  
本文件說明：**誰應該負責監測衛星再入造成的污染。**

目前情況是：

- 沒有國家被要求監測 Al₂O₃  
- 沒有國際機構負責追蹤再入污染  
- 衛星公司不需要提供排放資料  

這是全球治理中的一片「盲區」。

---

## 2. 為什麼監測很重要  
你無法管理你無法量測的東西。

若沒有監測：
- 不知道大氣中有多少 Al₂O₃  
- 不知道污染速度是否加快  
- 無法正確估算臭氧損失  
- 無法要求責任者負責  

在制定規範之前必須先建立監測系統。

---

## 3. 應該監測什麼？

### ● 1. 每次再入的 Al₂O₃ 排放量  
依衞星質量與材料估算。

### ● 2. 平流層微粒濃度  
使用地面 LIDAR、高空氣球或衛星遙測。

### ● 3. 臭氧厚度  
了解化學破壞程度。

### ● 4. 輻射強迫變化  
觀察微粒如何改變地球熱平衡。

### ● 5. 再入地點與頻率  
找出「污染熱點」。

---

## 4. 誰應該負責？（簡化責任框架）

| 單位 | 責任 |
|------|-------|
| **衛星公司** | 提供材料資料、報廢紀錄、再入時間 |
| **各國政府** | 監測領空、執行規範 |
| **太空機構（NASA/ESA/JAXA）** | 製作監測儀器與模型 |
| **國際機構（聯合國 COPUOS）** | 制定全球標準與申報程序 |
| **獨立科學家** | 審查資料真實性與透明度 |

這可避免責任落空。

---

## 5. 監測工具建議

### ✔ 地面 LIDAR  
偵測高空微粒濃度。

### ✔ 高空氣球  
直接採樣。

### ✔ 衛星遙測儀器  
監測全球臭氧與輻射平衡。

### ✔ 衛星公司強制申報制度  
簡單一條：  
**你把東西送上太空，就必須申報它再入時排放了什麼。**
