# Alternative Materials vs Aluminum  
替代材料 vs 鋁合金  
Space Reentry Environmental Jury · 02_Deorbit_and_Disposal

---

## 1. Background 背景
Aluminum is widely used in satellite structures due to:
- low density  
- high strength-to-weight ratio  
- machinability  
- cost efficiency  

But during re-entry:
- Al → Al₂O₃ nanoparticles  
- Major ozone-impacting agent  
- Long-lived aerosols  

---

## 2. Gemini Atmospheric Assessment  
### Emission Comparison (per satellite)
| Material | Re-entry Output | Ozone Impact | Lifetime |
|---------|-----------------|--------------|----------|
| Aluminum | ~30 kg Al₂O₃ | High | 5–10 years |
| Titanium | TiO₂ | Medium | 3–6 years |
| Carbon composites | Carbon dust | Low | 1–3 years |
| High-temp ceramics | Silicate dust | Very low | <1 year |

Gemini 結論：以碳纖維/複合材料取代鋁，可大幅降低污染。

---

## 3. Grok Engineering Assessment  
### Feasibility of Replacing Aluminum  
- Carbon composites: +5–10% cost, similar mass  
- Ceramics: +20–30% cost, brittle but heat-resistant  
- Titanium: strong but heavy and costly  
- Hybrid composite frames: TRL 7–8, ready for adoption  

### Engineering Trade-offs  
Replacing aluminum:
- increases cost slightly  
- reduces environmental impact drastically  
- improves thermal stability  
- reduces oxidation-based mass loss  

工程結論：可行且適合大型星鏈系統採用。
