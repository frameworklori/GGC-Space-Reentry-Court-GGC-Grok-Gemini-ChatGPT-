# Problem Statement: Satellite Re-entry & Environmental Impact
太空再入環境問題說明書

## 1. Background 背景
As satellite constellations expand into tens of thousands of units, the frequency of atmospheric re-entries has sharply increased. Each satellite burn-up injects metallic aerosols—including aluminum oxide (Al₂O₃), titanium oxides, and other combustion byproducts—into the mesosphere and stratosphere.

隨著數萬顆衛星進入軌道，衞星再入頻率急速增加。再入大氣層時的高溫燒蝕會產生金屬氣溶膠，包含：
- 氧化鋁微粒（Al₂O₃）
- 氧化鈦
- 其他高溫燃燒副產物

這些物質會累積於平流層與中氣層，可能改變臭氧化學與地球輻射平衡。

---

## 2. Core Problems 核心問題
### (1) Long-lived stratospheric aerosols  
Al₂O₃ particles persist for 5–10 years with minimal settling.

### (2) Ozone chemistry disruption  
Al₂O₃ surfaces catalyze heterogeneous reactions similar to polar stratospheric clouds.

### (3) Radiative forcing  
High-altitude aerosols alter solar scattering and infrared absorption.

### (4) Debris fallout risks  
5–10% of satellite mass may survive and reach Earth’s surface.

### (5) Lack of governance  
No global rule limits:
- total re-entry mass
- atmospheric damage
- debris liability
- launch quotas
