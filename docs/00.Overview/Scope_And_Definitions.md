*(English Version — Public-Friendly Edition)*

## 1. Purpose of This Document
This document clearly defines the scope, boundaries, and key terms used in the Space Reentry Environmental Jury.  
It ensures that all participants—AI jurors and the Human Main Judge—share the same understanding before analyzing specific cases.

---

## 2. Scope of the Space Reentry Environmental Jury
The Jury examines environmental and governance issues caused by **satellites reentering Earth’s atmosphere**.

### The Jury covers:
1. **Reentry Pollution**
   - Aluminum oxide (Al₂O₃) particles  
   - Titanium, magnesium, beryllium oxides  
   - NOx and other chemical byproducts  
   - Radiative forcing and ozone impact  

2. **Satellite End-of-Life Disposal Methods**
   - Passive burn-up in the atmosphere  
   - Controlled deorbiting  
   - Tug-assisted disposal  
   - Storage or “graveyard orbits”  
   - In-orbit recycling  

3. **Public Safety & Liability**
   - Surviving debris hitting people or property  
   - Insurance and compensation models  
   - Traceability of satellite fragments  

4. **Global Governance**
   - Launch quotas  
   - Multi-country approval for mega-constellations  
   - Monitoring responsibility for atmospheric pollution  
   - Environmental charges or taxes  

5. **Future Treaty Foundations**
   - Principles for global environmental responsibility  
   - Data-sharing requirements  
   - Minimum safety standards  

---

## 3. What Is *Not* Covered (Out-of-Scope)
To keep the deliberations focused, the Jury does **not** cover:

- Rocket launch pollution (handled separately)  
- Military space weapons  
- Asteroid mining  
- Deep-space missions outside Earth orbit  
- Planetary protection policies (Mars/Moon contamination)  

These are important topics but belong to other governance systems.

## 4. Key Definitions (Simple Explanations)

### ● Satellite Reentry  
The process of a satellite falling back into the atmosphere, heating up at ~7.8 km/s, and burning up.

### ● Aluminum Oxide (Al₂O₃) Particles  
White ceramic micro-particles created when aluminum burns during reentry.  
They stay in the upper atmosphere for years.

### ● Stratosphere  
A layer of the atmosphere 10–50 km above the surface, home to the ozone layer.

### ● Radiative Forcing  
A measure of how particles in the atmosphere change Earth’s energy balance—warming or cooling the planet.

### ● Tug-Assisted Removal  
A planned disposal method where a robotic “space tug” grabs old satellites and safely deorbits them.

### ● Graveyard Orbit  
A higher orbit where satellites are parked when no longer in use. Not suitable for LEO satellites.

### ● Environmental Liability  
Responsibility for damage caused by falling debris or atmospheric pollution.

## 1. 這份文件的目的
這份文件用來明確定義「太空再入環境陪審團」的審查範圍與核心用語。

## 2. 陪審團審查的範圍
陪審團專注於衞星**再入地球大氣時造成的環境與治理問題**。

### 本陪審團涵蓋：

1. **再入造成的污染**
   - 氧化鋁（Al₂O₃）微粒  
   - 鈦、鎂、鈹等金屬氧化物  
   - NOx 及其他化學副產物  
   - 輻射強迫、臭氧層影響  

2. **衞星生命週期終端（EOL）處理方式**
   - 被動自然燒毀  
   - 可控降軌  
   - 太空拖船（tug）協助處理  
   - 高軌道停放（graveyard orbit）  
   - 軌道內拆解與材料回收  

3. **公共安全與賠償責任**
   - 衛星碎片砸傷人或破壞財物  
   - 保險與賠償制度  
   - 碎片的來源追溯標記  

4. **全球治理問題**
   - 發射數量限制  
   - 多國同意的星鏈部署制度  
   - 平流層污染的監測責任  
   - 排放費、環境費制度  

5. **未來條約建構**
   - 全球環境責任框架  
   - 資料共享義務  
   - 最低安全標準  

---

## 3. 不屬於本陪審團的議題（排除範圍）
為保持專注，本陪審團不討論：

- 火箭發射造成的污染（屬於另一議題）  
- 軍事空間武器  
- 小行星採礦  
- 地球軌道以外的深空任務  
- 行星保護政策（火星、月球微生物污染）  

這些議題很重要，但不屬於本陪審團工作。

## 4. 核心定義（大眾化說明）

### ● 衞星再入  
衞星以約 7.8 km/s 的速度掉入大氣層並因摩擦高熱而燒毀。

### ● 氧化鋁（Al₂O₃）微粒  
鋁在高溫燒毀後形成的白色陶瓷微粒，能在平流層待好幾年。

### ● 平流層  
位於 10–50 公里，臭氧層就在這裡。

### ● 輻射強迫  
衡量大氣顆粒如何改變地球吸收或反射熱量，影響氣候。

### ● 太空拖船（Tug）  
可以抓取報廢衞星並帶其安全降軌的太空機器人。

### ● 墓地軌道（Graveyard Orbit）  
讓衞星退休的高軌道，不適用於 LEO 衛星。

### ● 環境責任  
再入碎片造成損害或污染的責任歸屬。
