# LORI Space Environment Charter v1.0  
*(English Version — Public-Friendly Edition)*

---

## 1. Purpose of the Charter

The **LORI Space Environment Charter** establishes a human-centered, ethical framework for:

- Satellite launches  
- Reentry emissions  
- Debris responsibility  
- Orbital safety  
- Long-term space sustainability  

It ensures that **Earth’s atmosphere and orbital space remain safe for future generations**.

The Charter follows a simple principle:

> **No company or country may use space in a way that harms Earth or humanity.**

---

## 2. Core Principles

### **1. Human Sovereignty First**  
AI provides advice.  
Humans make all final decisions.

### **2. Space as Shared Heritage**  
Orbital space and the stratosphere belong to no single nation.

### **3. Polluter Responsibility**  
Those who cause pollution or debris must contribute to mitigation.

### **4. Transparency and Data Sharing**  
Reentry plans and collision-risk data must be public or reviewable.

### **5. Sustainability Over Profit**  
Business growth cannot override atmospheric protection.

### **6. Fair Access**  
All countries should have a chance to access space, not only superpowers.

### **7. Future Generations Included**  
Policies must consider 50–100 year impacts, not just short-term profits.

---

## 3. Requirements for Satellite Operators

### ✔ Clear end-of-life (EOL) plan  
No mission may launch without a safe disposal method.

### ✔ Full materials disclosure  
Operators must reveal what metals and fuels are used.

### ✔ Reentry impact assessment  
Pollution levels must be modeled and reviewed.

### ✔ Collision-risk transparency  
Data must be shareable with global tracking networks.

### ✔ Insurance and compensation  
Victims on Earth must be protected from debris damage.

### ✔ Participation in global environmental funds  
Operators must contribute to the Global Space Environmental Fund (GSEF).

---

## 4. International Obligations for Governments

- Approve launches only if reentry pollution is within limits  
- Participate in multi-country consent for mega-constellations  
- Share atmospheric monitoring data  
- Enforce penalties for irresponsible operators  
- Support cleanup missions and science research  

Governments cannot allow companies to damage space or Earth unchecked.

# LORI 太空環境憲章 v1.0  
*(中文版本 — 大眾閱讀版)*

---

## 1. 制定目的

**LORI 太空環境憲章**建立一個以人類為核心的太空使用倫理框架：

- 衞星發射  
- 再入污染  
- 碎片責任  
- 軌道安全  
- 長期永續太空治理  

其目標是：

> **確保地球大氣層與太空環境，對未來世代保持安全。**

---

## 2. 憲章核心原則

### **1. 人類主權優先**  
AI 提供建議，最終決策由人類掌握。

### **2. 太空屬於全球共享**  
不屬於任何單一國家或公司。

### **3. 污染者負責**  
製造污染或碎片者需承擔治理成本。

### **4. 資料透明與共享**  
再入計畫、碰撞風險需可審查。

### **5. 永續優先於商業利益**  
發展不能犧牲環境。

### **6. 公平使用**  
所有國家皆應有機會進入太空。

### **7. 以未來為考量**  
政策需考慮未來 50～100 年的影響。

---

## 3. 衞星營運者的義務

### ✔ 明確生命週期終點計畫  
沒有 EOL，不准發射。

### ✔ 材料完整揭露  
金屬與推進劑必須透明申報。

### ✔ 再入污染評估  
需提供完整模型。

### ✔ 碰撞風險透明  
必須與全球追蹤網合作。

### ✔ 事故保險與補償  
地球受害者不應自行承擔風險。

### ✔ 參與全球環境基金（GSEF）  
所有太空使用者需負擔最低保護成本。

---

## 4. 國家政府的義務

- 僅批准符合環境門檻的發射  
- 多國共同審查大型星鏈  
- 共享大氣監測資料  
- 懲處不負責任的營運者  
- 支援太空垃圾清除與研究  

政府不能任由企業破壞環境。
