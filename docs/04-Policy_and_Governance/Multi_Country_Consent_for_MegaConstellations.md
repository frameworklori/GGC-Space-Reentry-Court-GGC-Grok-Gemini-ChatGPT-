# Multi-Country Consent for Mega-Constellations  
*(English Version — Public-Friendly Edition)*

---

## 1. What Is “Multi-Country Consent”?

A **mega-constellation** is a network of thousands of satellites operated by a single company or country.

Examples:
- Starlink  
- Kuiper  
- OneWeb  
- China’s GW constellation  

These systems affect **every country**, because:

- They occupy global orbital space  
- Their reentry pollution spreads across borders  
- Collisions create debris that threaten all satellites  
- Their radio spectrum use impacts global communication  

“Multi-country consent” means:

> **A large satellite network should not be approved by one country alone.  
Nations must jointly agree before deployment.**

---

## 2. Why Is This Necessary?

Because mega-constellations create **global externalities**:

### ✔ Pollution spreads worldwide  
Al₂O₃ particles stay in the stratosphere for years.

### ✔ Risk of collisions increases for all nations  
More satellites = more debris.

### ✔ Vulnerable nations have no say  
Small countries are affected but cannot regulate.

### ✔ Orbital space is limited  
If one actor dominates, others are locked out.

### ✔ Prevents “legal loopholes”  
Currently, a company only needs approval from **its own country**.

This system is outdated for a world with 100,000+ satellites.

---

## 3. How Multi-Country Consent Could Work

### **Option A — Minimum Approval Rule**  
A mega-constellation requires approval from:
- The operator’s country  
- + 3–5 independent nations  
- + International space-environment review board

### **Option B — Regional Consent**  
Approval from:
- Spacefaring nations  
- Neighboring orbital users  
- Countries under reentry paths  

### **Option C — Atmospheric Impact Review**  
Approval depends on:
- Pollution modeling  
- Ozone impact thresholds  
- Collision-risk modeling  

Consent is denied if environmental limits are exceeded.

---

## 4. What Happens If Countries Disagree?

- Operators must revise their plan  
- Reduce number of satellites  
- Improve materials  
- Provide compensation or environmental guarantees  
- Submit new reentry strategy  

No single nation may “go ahead alone.”

---

## 5. Benefits of Multi-Country Consent

### ✔ Protects Earth’s atmosphere  
Shared review reduces pollution.

### ✔ Protects orbital safety  
More eyes = fewer catastrophic risks.

### ✔ Protects global fairness  
Big companies cannot monopolize orbital space.

### ✔ Encourages transparency  
International review prevents hidden environmental impacts.

# 大型星鏈「多國共同同意」制度  
*(中文版本 — 大眾閱讀版)*

---

## 1. 什麼是「多國共同同意」制度？

所謂 **大型星鏈（mega-constellation）** 指：

- 上千至上萬顆衞星  
- 由同一家公司或國家營運  
- 在全球軌道上大規模運作  

這種系統影響 **每一個國家**，因為：

- 佔據全球軌道  
- 再入污染會跨國擴散  
- 碰撞碎片影響所有衞星  
- 電磁頻譜使用具有全球性影響  

「多國共同同意」的精神是：

> **大型星鏈不能只經過單一國家批准，必須由多國共同審查後才能部署。**

---

## 2. 為什麼需要多國審查？

因為大型星鏈帶來的後果是全球性的：

### ✔ 污染跨國界擴散  
氧化鋁微粒在平流層能飄多年。

### ✔ 碰撞風險是全球共同承擔  
越多衞星，越容易連鎖碰撞。

### ✔ 弱勢國家無法自保  
小國沒有太空法規能力。

### ✔ 軌道資源有限  
被佔滿後，小國或新創無法進入。

### ✔ 避免法律漏洞  
現在只要本國批准就能發射——這不合理。

---

## 3. 多國同意可以如何設計？

### **方案 A — 最低同意門檻**  
需獲得以下批准：

- 本國  
- + 3 至 5 個獨立國家  
- + 國際太空環境審查委員會  

### **方案 B — 區域審查制度**  
需獲：

- 主要太空國家  
- 軌道鄰國  
- 可能受再入影響的國家  
- 環境安全審查  

### **方案 C — 大氣影響審查**  
若污染模型超標，則不得批准。

---

## 4. 若有國家反對怎麼辦？

- 要求營運商改善  
- 減少衞星數量  
- 改善材料與再入設計  
- 提供保險或環境保護方案  
- 修改後重新送審  

任何國家無法「自行批准然後強行部署」。

---

## 5. 多國共同同意的好處

### ✔ 減少全球大氣風險  
共同把關污染。

### ✔ 降低太空碰撞風險  
多國監督更嚴謹。

### ✔ 保護全球公平  
大公司不能獨佔太空資源。

### ✔ 提升透明度  
避免藏匿環境風險。
