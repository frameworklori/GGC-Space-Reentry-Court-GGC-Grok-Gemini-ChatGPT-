# Launch Quota and Licensing  
*(English Version — Public-Friendly Edition)*

---

## 1. Why Do We Need Launch Quotas?

Satellite launches are increasing rapidly:

- Mega-constellations  
- Thousands of small satellites  
- Reentry pollution  
- Collision risks  
- Debris multiplication  

Space is becoming crowded.

Without rules, this can lead to:

- Uncontrolled atmospheric pollution  
- Orbital congestion and Kessler syndrome  
- Increased debris collisions  
- Unfair advantage for a few dominant companies  
- Environmental harm that affects all countries  

A launch quota system sets **fair, science-based limits** on how many satellites can be launched per year.

---

## 2. What Is a Launch Quota System?

A launch quota system regulates:

- How many satellites each country or company can launch  
- How many satellites can be in orbit at once  
- What environmental requirements must be met before launching  
- How to prioritize safety over speed

It is similar to:

- Fishing quotas (to prevent ocean depletion)  
- Emission quotas (to prevent climate damage)  

The idea is not to stop innovation — but to **prevent harmful overgrowth**.

---

## 3. What Factors Determine the Quota?

A global or national quota can be based on:

### ✔ Satellite mass and size  
Heavy satellites produce more debris and more pollution.

### ✔ Reentry plan  
Operators with cleaner disposal plans get higher quotas.

### ✔ Material choice  
Aluminum-heavy satellites may receive stricter limits.

### ✔ Collision risk  
Crowded orbits get tighter restrictions.

### ✔ Country contribution to monitoring  
Nations that invest in tracking and cleanup may earn higher allowances.

### ✔ Environmental performance  
Using low-pollution materials or controlled reentry earns quota credits.

The system rewards **responsibility**, not just money.

---

## 4. Licensing Requirements

Every satellite operator must obtain a launch license that proves:

### **1. The satellite has a clear end-of-life plan**  
“No plan, no launch.”  
This prevents uncontrolled reentry pollution.

### **2. Materials are environmentally reasonable**  
Encourages alternatives to aluminum-based designs.

### **3. Collision-risk analysis is complete**  
Requires data sharing with global tracking networks.

### **4. Insurance or compensation coverage is in place**  
Victims on Earth should not pay for space accidents.

### **5. Compliance with global quotas**  
Ensures fairness and prevents overloading specific orbits.

---

## 5. How Quotas Improve the Space Ecosystem

### ✔ Slows down atmospheric damage  
Less uncontrolled reentry = fewer aluminum oxide particles.

### ✔ Reduces debris risk  
Better tracking, fewer collisions.

### ✔ Makes companies innovate responsibly  
They must improve materials and design to qualify.

### ✔ Protects smaller nations  
Prevents monopoly over orbital real estate.

### ✔ Supports long-term sustainability  
Avoids repeating Earth’s pollution mistakes in space.

# 發射配額與發射許可制度  
*(中文版本 — 大眾閱讀版)*

---

## 1. 為什麼需要「發射配額」？

衞星發射量正急速增加：

- 大型星鏈  
- 成千上萬小型衞星  
- 再入污染  
- 碰撞風險  
- 太空垃圾增生危機  

太空正在變得擁擠。

如果沒有規範，將導致：

- 平流層污染失控  
- 軌道擁塞、Kessler 連鎖碰撞  
- 衛星墜落風險增加  
- 少數公司壟斷軌道資源  
- 全球共同環境受到威脅  

發射配額制度的目的，就是以 **公平、科學** 的標準限制每年可以發射的衞星數量。

---

## 2. 什麼是「發射配額制度」？

發射配額制度調控：

- 每個國家／公司每年能發射多少顆衞星  
- 軌道能容納多少衞星  
- 什麼條件才能取得發射許可  
- 優先確保安全與永續，而不是盲目擴張  

類似：

- 海洋捕魚配額（保護過度捕撈）  
- 污染排放配額（降低環境破壞）  

不是阻止發展，而是避免 **過度成長造成危機**。

---

## 3. 配額的決定因素

全球或國家級配額可依：

### ✔ 衛星重量與尺寸  
越大 = 風險越高。

### ✔ 再入計畫  
有清晰再入路徑者可取得更高配額。

### ✔ 材料選擇  
使用高污染材料（如鋁）者會被更嚴格限制。

### ✔ 碰撞風險  
擁擠軌道的配額更低。

### ✔ 國家對監測的貢獻  
投入監控與清理技術的國家可獲更高配額。

### ✔ 環境績效  
可控再入、低污染材料可獲得額外加分。

制度不以金錢決定，而以 **責任與永續** 為核心。

---

## 4. 發射許可的要求

所有營運商都需取得「發射許可證」，並必須證明：

### **1. 衛星有明確的生命週期終點計畫**  
沒有再入計畫，就不准發射。

### **2. 材料符合環保最低標準**  
鼓勵減少鋁材、使用替代材料。

### **3. 完成碰撞風險分析**  
與全球追蹤網共享資料。

### **4. 有足夠保險或補償機制**  
不能讓地球上的受害者自行承擔損失。

### **5. 遵守國際發射配額**  
確保公平，杜絕過度佔用軌道。

---

## 5. 配額制度帶來的好處

### ✔ 降低大氣污染  
再入數量下降，氧化鋁減少。

### ✔ 減少太空碎片  
發射更受控、設計更嚴謹。

### ✔ 促進企業創新  
若要取得配額，就必須打造更乾淨的衞星。

### ✔ 保護弱勢國家  
避免少數國家壟斷軌道資源。

### ✔ 建立長期永續性  
避免把地球的錯誤重複在太空。
