## 1. Background 背景  
Although most satellites fully burn up during re-entry, **5–10% of structural mass may survive** and reach Earth’s surface as metallic debris.  
Risks include:
- injury or fatality  
- property damage  
- infrastructure disruption  
- airline interception hazards  

雖然大多數衞星會完全燒蝕，但仍有 **5–10% 金屬碎片** 可能落至地面，造成傷害或財損。

目前保險制度無法涵蓋大部分跨國責任。

### 2.2 Probability  
NASA/ESA models suggest casualty risk of:
- **1 in 10,000** per uncontrolled re-entry  
- With megaconstellations: rising to **1 in 5,000**

# 3. Grok Engineering Assessment  
工程陪審員：Grok

### Causes of surviving debris  
- titanium pressure vessels  
- stainless steel tanks  
- reaction wheels  
- heat-resistant ceramics  
- incomplete breakup at lower altitudes  

### Traceability Issues  
- No global requirement for RFID tagging  
- No universal “black box” for satellites  
- No telemetry record of last moments  

工程結論：追查責任非常困難，需要技術化的識別機制。

# 4. ChatGPT Governance Assessment  
治理陪審員：ChatGPT

### Legal Vacuum  
- Liability Convention (1972) only covers damage on Earth, **not atmospheric harm**  
- “Fault-based liability” for space = nearly impossible to prove  
- No global compensation pool  
- Victims usually cannot identify which company’s satellite caused the injury  

### Policy Recommendations  
- Establish **Strict Liability Standard**  
- Require each satellite to include:
  - RFID ID tags  
  - thermal-resistant serial plates  
  - debris traceability modules  
- Create **international space debris insurance pool**  
- Compensation scale based on kinetic energy + mass  

治理結論：路徑清晰，僅需國際協調落地。
