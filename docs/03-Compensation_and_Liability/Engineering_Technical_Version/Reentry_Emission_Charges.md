# Reentry Emission Charges  
*(English Version — Public-Friendly Edition)*

---

## 1. What Are “Reentry Emission Charges”?

When a satellite reenters Earth’s atmosphere, it burns up and releases:

- Aluminum oxide (Al₂O₃) particles  
- Metal vapors (Mg, Ti, Fe)  
- Combustion-related gases (NOx, etc.)

These materials stay in the **stratosphere for years**, affecting:

- Ozone chemistry  
- Radiative balance  
- Polar climate  
- Cloud formation patterns

Reentry Emission Charges are a proposed system where companies pay a fee based on **how much pollution their satellite creates during re-entry**.

The goal is simple:

> “Polluters pay their fair share — and fund atmospheric protection.”

---

## 2. Why Do We Need an Emission Fee?

Right now:

- Launch fees exist  
- Collision liability exists  
- But **atmospheric pollution from reentry is not priced at all**

This loophole means:

- Companies can launch thousands of satellites  
- Each one adds to stratospheric pollution  
- And **nobody pays for the environmental cost**

Without a pricing model, pollution is “free.”

Reentry emission charges close this gap.

---

## 3. How Emission Charges Could Work

A basic model:

### **Fee = Pollution Mass × Impact Factor × Risk Region**

Where:

- **Pollution Mass** = kg of Al₂O₃ and other particles generated  
- **Impact Factor** = scientific estimate of ozone damage or radiative forcing  
- **Risk Region** = whether reentry happens near sensitive areas (polar vortex, populated zones)

Example (public-friendly numbers):

| Satellite Type | Typical Al₂O₃ Released | Estimated Fee |
|----------------|------------------------|---------------|
| SmallSat (100 kg) | 10–12 kg | $1,500–$3,000 |
| Starlink-class (250 kg) | 25–30 kg | $3,000–$7,000 |
| Large satellite (>1,000 kg) | 60–100 kg | $10,000–$25,000 |

Note: Fees are proportional, not punitive.

---

## 4. What Would the Money Be Used For?

A global fund could support:

- Atmospheric monitoring (LIDAR, satellites, balloons)  
- Ozone recovery research  
- Cleanup missions for orbital debris  
- Compensation mechanisms for debris-related incidents  
- Climate impact mitigation

This ensures the fee is **not a tax**, but a **protection fund**.

---

## 5. Why This Benefits the Industry Too

Surprisingly, emission fees can help satellite companies:

### ✔ Builds public trust  
### ✔ Provides regulatory clarity  
### ✔ Encourages greener satellite design  
### ✔ Reduces future legal and political conflicts  
### ✔ Stabilizes the long-term space economy  

If companies contribute to the solution, they gain legitimacy and avoid harsher bans later.

# 再入排放費用  
*(中文版本 — 大眾閱讀版)*

---

## 1. 什麼是「再入排放費用」？

衞星再入地球大氣層時會產生：

- 氧化鋁（Al₂O₃）微粒  
- 金屬蒸氣（如鎂、鈦、鐵）  
- 氮氧化物（NOx）及其他大氣化學物質  

這些物質會在 **平流層停留數年甚至十年**，影響：

- 臭氧層  
- 輻射平衡  
- 極地氣候系統  
- 雲層形成與地面溫度  

「再入排放費」的核心概念是：

> **誰造成排放，誰負擔部分環境成本。**

---

## 2. 為什麼需要排放費？

目前地球上：

- 有發射費  
- 有碰撞責任  
- 但 **對「再入造成的空氣污染」完全沒有收費**

這造成一個巨大漏洞：

- 公司可以自由發射大量衞星  
- 再入造成的污染沒人負責  
- 地球大氣層變成免費垃圾場  

排放費就是為了補上這個空缺。

---

## 3. 排放費可能如何計算？

簡單模型：

### **費用 = 污染質量 × 影響係數 × 風險區域**

其中：

- **污染質量**：再入時產生的 Al₂O₃ 與其他微粒的重量  
- **影響係數**：臭氧耗損、輻射強迫等科學評估  
- **風險區域**：是否落在敏感區，如極地渦旋  

示意表：

| 衞星類型 | 氧化鋁排放量 | 估計費用 |
|----------|--------------|----------|
| 小型衞星（100kg） | 10–12 kg | $1,500–$3,000 |
| Starlink 級（250kg） | 25–30 kg | $3,000–$7,000 |
| 大型衞星（>1000kg） | 60–100 kg | $10,000–$25,000 |

以上費用僅作示意。

---

## 4. 這些費用會用在哪裡？

可建立全球基金，用於：

- 大氣監測（LIDAR、平流層氣球、監測衞星）  
- 臭氧研究與氣候模型  
- 太空垃圾清除技術  
- 再入碎片傷害補償基金  
- 空間環境治理計畫  

這筆費用不是稅，而是 **地球保護費**。

---

## 5. 為什麼企業其實也會受益？

排放費制度有助於：

✔ 建立企業可信度  
✔ 讓監管更可預測  
✔ 促進使用更環保的材料  
✔ 避免未來更嚴厲的禁令  
✔ 維持太空產業長期運作  

企業付的是成本，得到的是 **未來生存空間**。
