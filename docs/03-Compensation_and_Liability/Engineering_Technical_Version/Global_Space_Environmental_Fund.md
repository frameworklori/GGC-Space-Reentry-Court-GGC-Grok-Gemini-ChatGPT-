# Global Space Environmental Fund  
*(English Version — Public-Friendly Edition)*

---

## 1. What Is the Global Space Environmental Fund?

As satellite numbers grow rapidly, reentry emissions and debris risks are becoming a shared global problem.

A **Global Space Environmental Fund (GSEF)** is a public-interest financial pool designed to:

- Monitor atmospheric pollution caused by satellite reentry  
- Support countries affected by falling debris  
- Finance cleanup missions  
- Encourage greener satellite design  
- Provide scientific funding for ozone and climate impact studies  

In simple terms:

> “Everyone who uses space contributes a small amount to keep space and Earth safe.”

---

## 2. Why Do We Need This Fund?

### Because the space environment is shared by *every country*.

But today:

- Satellite operators benefit economically  
- Pollution enters the stratosphere  
- Debris risks affect all nations  
- Only a few countries have monitoring systems  
- There is no global financial mechanism for environmental protection  

A worldwide fund ensures:

- Shared responsibility  
- Fair cost distribution  
- Protection for countries with fewer resources  
- Long-term stability of the space economy  

---

## 3. How Would the Fund Be Financed?

Possible funding sources:

### ✔ Launch Contribution  
Each rocket launch contributes a small, fixed fee.

### ✔ Reentry Emission Charges  
A percentage of the pollution fee (from the previous section) goes into the fund.

### ✔ Satellite License Fees  
Operators of large constellations pay annual sustainability fees.

### ✔ Voluntary National Contributions  
Countries may provide additional support.

### ✔ Private/NGO Partnerships  
Environmental groups or scientific foundations may co-fund monitoring systems.

The cost to each company remains small but becomes significant when pooled globally.

---

## 4. How Would the Fund Be Used?

### **1. Atmospheric Monitoring Network**  
- LIDAR systems  
- High-altitude balloons  
- Monitoring satellites  
- Ozone/Al₂O₃ concentration studies  

### **2. Emergency Assistance for Debris Impact**  
If debris injures people or damages property:

- The fund pays compensation quickly  
- Victims do not need to navigate complex space laws  
- Countries with fewer resources receive support  

### **3. Funding Cleanup Missions**  
Supports tugs, recycling, or experimental cleanup systems.

### **4. Scientific Research**  
- Ozone recovery research  
- Radiative forcing analysis  
- Long-term climate models  

### **5. Global Governance Development**  
Funds treaty discussions, audits, and global coordination.

# 全球太空環境基金  
*(中文版本 — 大眾閱讀版)*

---

## 1. 什麼是「全球太空環境基金」？

隨著衞星越來越多，再入污染與碎片風險已成為全球共同問題。

**全球太空環境基金（GSEF）** 是一個公益性基金，旨在：

- 監測衞星再入造成的大氣污染  
- 補償碎片傷害造成的損失  
- 支援太空垃圾清除計畫  
- 鼓勵更環保的衞星設計  
- 資助臭氧與氣候科學研究  

一句話：

> 「使用太空的人，一起負擔一點成本，讓地球和太空更安全。」

---

## 2. 為什麼需要這個基金？

因為太空環境是 **全人類共享的**。

目前的問題是：

- 衞星營運者獲利  
- 污染卻進入平流層  
- 碎片墜落可能傷害任何國家  
- 多數國家沒有監測設備  
- 世界上沒有一個共同資助的保護機制  

全球基金可確保：

- 責任共享  
- 費用公平  
- 保護資源較少的國家  
- 維持太空經濟的長期穩定  

---

## 3. 基金的資金來源

可能由以下構成：

### ✔ 發射貢獻金  
每次火箭發射支付固定費用。

### ✔ 再入排放費  
排放費的一部分納入基金。

### ✔ 衞星營運年費  
大型星鏈公司支付年度永續費。

### ✔ 國家捐助  
由各國自由額外投入。

### ✔ 民間與 NGO 合作  
環保組織、研究基金會可共同資助。

費用對每家公司都很低，但全球累積起來十分可觀。

---

## 4. 基金的用途

### **1. 大氣監測網**  
- LIDAR  
- 平流層氣球  
- 監測衞星  
- 臭氧／氧化鋁研究  

### **2. 碎片墜落事故補償**  
當碎片傷人或砸毀房屋：

- 基金可立即賠付  
- 不必等待法律程序  
- 資源不足的國家也能獲得支援  

### **3. 支援太空垃圾清除技術**  
如拖船、回收站、清除計畫等。

### **4. 科學研究**  
- 臭氧修復研究  
- 輻射強迫模型  
- 長期氣候模擬  

### **5. 全球治理費用**  
資助條約談判、國際監測、環境評估。

---

## 5. 為什麼所有人都會受益？

### 對產業  
- 法規更可預測  
- 公眾信任提升  
- 避免未來的嚴厲限制  

### 對政府  
- 碎片事故快速補償  
- 大氣數據更精準  
- 共同負擔更公平  

### 對人類  
- 更乾淨的大氣層  
- 更安全的太空  
- 長期可持續發展
